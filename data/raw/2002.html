Notes:
- KR are champions for the 23th time but it is the first time ever that 
  the club won the title at their home ground, KR-völlur, which they have 
  played at since 1983. In 1999 they won the title in 17th round at 
  Laugardalsvöllur (away vs Víkingur, 0-4) and 2000 they won at Stjörnuvöllur 
  in 18th round (away vs Stjarnan, 1-4). KR did not win the title 1969 to 1998.
- Sigursteinn Gíslason won the title for the 8th time as a player which is 
  a record for the ten-team league (1977-2002). Sigusteinn Gíslason won the 
  title with ÍA (1992, 1993, 1994, 1995, 1996) and then with KR (1999, 2000 
  and 2002). He did own the old record with Ólafur Þórðarson who won the title 
  with ÍA (1983, 1984, 1993, 1994, 1995, 1996 and finally in 2001 as a 
  player-coach).
- Fylkir lost the title in the final rounds for the third year running. Fylkir 
  has been in the first place for 25 of the 52 rounds in seasons 2000-2002 but 
  never after the 18th round.
- Willum Þór Þórsson, the KR coach became the first coach in Iceland football 
  history to win all the four levels. Willum Þór Þórsson did coach Þróttur 
  (won 2nd level 1997), Haukar (won 4th level 2000 and 3rd level 2001) and 
  now KR (won 1st level, 2002).
